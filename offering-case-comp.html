Framework Recall Under Pressure

Knowing a framework and accessing it calmly in a 30-minute case interview are different skills. 4 timed rounds build the recall muscle - not the reading-about-it muscle.

Real-Time Mentor Feedback

After each round, your mentor identifies exactly where your structure broke, where you over-complicated, and where you rushed. Not a rubric - a conversation from someone who sat in that interviewer's seat.

Presentation Confidence

Case competitions test how you present your analysis - not just the analysis. Deck structure, verbal walk-throughs, Q&A handling - all practiced and corrected before your real interviews.

Program Structure

4 Rounds. Progressive Difficulty.

Each round builds on the last. By round 4, you're solving the same type of cases that appear in Consulting final rounds.

01
Framework Foundations

Introduction to case types (market entry, profitability, M&A, operations). Framework mapping and structured approach - building the vocabulary before the pressure.

Week 1
02
Timed Case Round

First live case under time pressure. 30-minute limit, real-time mentor observation. Feedback on structure clarity, hypothesis framing, and question quality.

Week 2
03
Presentation Round

A case delivered as a structured presentation - the format used in Consulting finals and strategy roles. Deck structure, verbal walk-through, and Q&A handling.

Week 3
04
Final Mock Round

Full-length simulation of a final Consulting round case interview. Difficult case type. Full debrief and summary feedback document. Personal performance report provided.

Who This Is For

Profile A
"I know the frameworks. I just can't use them under pressure."

Reading about MECE and doing a timed case in front of a Kearney alumni are two entirely different experiences. This program bridges that gap through repetition and progressive pressure.

Profile B
"I'm targeting Consulting or Strategy - it's a core requirement."

Case interviews are not optional in Consulting recruiting. Every top firm - from Accenture Strategy to Kearney to boutique strategy houses - screens through structured cases. This isn't supplementary prep; it's the test.

Profile C
"I practice with peers but never get feedback that's actually useful."

Peer case practice is better than nothing. But your peers can't tell you where your structure broke compared to what they've seen in real interviews. A mentor who placed at Accenture Strategy can - and that difference in feedback quality is what changes outcomes.
